How Email Works

Duration: 50-65 minutes

Email Journey

Composition

When you write an email, your email client formats it according to specific standards and prepares it for transmission.

Transmission

The email is sent through SMTP (Simple Mail Transfer Protocol) servers, which relay it to its destination.

Delivery

The recipient's mail server receives the email and stores it until the recipient's email client retrieves it.

Email Protocols

SMTP

Simple Mail Transfer Protocol handles the sending and routing of email between servers.

POP3

Post Office Protocol downloads email to your device and typically deletes it from the server.

IMAP

Internet Message Access Protocol keeps emails on the server and syncs across devices.

Email Security

Encryption

TLS (Transport Layer Security) encrypts emails in transit to protect them from interception.

Authentication

SPF, DKIM, and DMARC help verify that emails come from legitimate senders.

Spam Filtering

Advanced algorithms analyze emails to detect and filter out unwanted spam messages.
